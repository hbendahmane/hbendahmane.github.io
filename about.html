Who I am & What I aim to become

Born and raised in a French working-class neighborhood, I grew up immersed in values of hard work, excellence, and perseverance. Passionate about these values and culture in all its diversity, I have dreamed since childhood of intellectual, professional, and social advancement.

I fought hard to gain admission to the most prestigious national scientific institutions. After five years of intense study, I am proud to hold the French title of “Ingénieur,” with two master’s degrees.

Still driven by my characteristic thirst for excellence, and with greater maturity, I am now ready to enter the professional world or pursue a doctorate with conviction.

With a rigorous general engineering background in physics, mathematics, chemistry, computer science, and biology, I hope to put my skills to use and develop them further in the service of global issues. These may include natural hazards, global warming, and pollution.

1. Luctor et emergo (“I struggle and I emerge”) is the historic motto of the Dutch province of Zeeland (16th century), symbolizing perseverance and resilience in the face of adversity. It is also the title of Ed Dean’s 2010 book Luctor et Emergo: Latin for I Struggle and Overcome, where the expression conveys resilience in life’s struggles and the pursuit of one’s goals.
